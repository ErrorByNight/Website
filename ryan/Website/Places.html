Robert Burns

GPS coordinates 55.0661° N, 3.6081° W

It was built by his father, William Burns in 1757. Burns, Scotland's national poet, was born there on 25 January 1759. It is a simple two-roomed clay and thatch cottage and has been fully restored to become part of Robert Burns Birthplace Museum. The museum has a bust of Burns by Patric Park.

Scotland’s national bard spent his last years in this simple sandstone house that is a key pilgrimage site for Burns' enthusiast from around the world. Over the years it has attracted many famous visitors including the poets Wordsworth, Coleridge and Keats. View original manuscripts and some of Burns’ personal belongings. See his desk and chair in the study where he created some of his finest works.

Caerlaverock Castle

GPS coordinates 54.9757° N, 3.5240° W

A wide moat, twin-towered gatehouse and lofty battlements make Caerlaverock the epitome of the medieval stronghold. Learn all about its turbulent history as a castle caught up in border conflicts.

Its fairytale appearance means the castle is also a popular filming location. Caerlaverock features in The Decoy Bride, a romantic comedy starring David Tennant and Kelly Macdonald.

ANNANDALE DISTILLERY

GPS coordinates 55.0016° N, 3.2556° W

Annandale Distillery has been through three whisky eras. Whilst in the ownership of the famous Johnnie Walker, Annandale Distillery closed in 1918. Rescued by David Thomson & Teresa Church in 2007, this once derelict distillery sprang back into life on November 3rd, 2014. Learn about the history of Annandale Distillery and how we produce fine Single Malt Scotch Whisky, and finish with a taste of Annandale’s exceptional whiskies.

Drumlanrig Castle

GPS coordinates 55.2737° N, 3.8089° W

Immerse yourself in history at majestic Drumlanrig Castle. The finely hewn red sandstone that gives the ‘Pink Palace’ its affectionate name, adds to the beauty of what is regarded as one of the finest examples of 17th Century Renaissance architecture in Scotland. As you stand beneath the Castle’s imposing façade you’ll take in the same breath taking views across the Nith Valley that convinced the first Duke of Queensberry to build his ancestral home here.

Gretna Gateway

GPS coordinates 55.9967° N, 3.0569° W

Located right on the border between England and Scotland, Gretna Gateway is the ideal place to stop on your journey. Romantic and historic Gretna and Gretna Green are known the world over for their weddings (up to 4,000 or more weddings per year, in a village with a population of less than 4,000!). Weddings still take place on a daily basis at various wedding venues including the registrar’s office and The Old Blacksmith’s Shop Visitor Centre.

The historic city of Carlisle is just eight miles down the road and the World Heritage Site of Hadrian’s Wall and the stunning scenery of The Lake District are a mere 30-60 minutes’ drive away, so there’s plenty to do and see. Also, Dumfries and Galloway, often referred to as Scotland’s best-kept secret, offers a unique blend of fabulous scenery, glorious beaches, walks and cycling which can be as leisurely or as arduous as you choose, and peaceful tranquillity which is difficult to surpass.

The Mill on the Fleet

GPS coordinates 54.8824° N, 4.1855° W

The Mill on the Fleet is a restored cotton mill dating from 1785 which is now an exhibition centre. It is attractively situated on the banks of the River Fleet with a long riverside terrace as part of the dog-friendly Mill Café. The exhibitions are spread over 3 floors telling the story of Gatehouse and the surrounding area and includes a temporary exhibition programme, regular events, and local arts and crafts. On the top floor there is a very popular second-hand bookshop. It is also the main tourist information point for Gatehouse. It is a community project run by volunteers - we look forward to welcoming you.
