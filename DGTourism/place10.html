About the area "Stranraer"


Facts: Stranraer is the administrative centre for the West Galloway Wigtownshire area of the County. The town is known for the ferry ports connecting N.Ireland and Scotland, and allowing trade to take place. For things to do in Stranraer why not try Agnew Park , Castle Kennedy Gardens, and the bontanical gardens.



History: In 1307, the Battle of Loch Ryan was fought near Stranraer, during the Scottish Wars of Indeendence. The invasion force was quickly overwhelmed by local forces, led by Dungal MacDouall. All the leaders were captured. In 1600 Stranraer has become the market town for wester Wigtownshire. At this time Stranraer had been reached by a military road built from Dumfries to allow easier access, this would become the A75, that stretched from Gretna to Stranraer.



Statistics: The population of Stranraer is 10'851
