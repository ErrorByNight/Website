Newton Stewart

54.9603° N, 4.4832° W


About the area "Newton Stewart"


Facts: The main local industries are: Agriculture, Forestry & Tourism. The town hosts a local market to help support the local farming industry. Newton Stewart lies on the southern edge of the Galloway Forest Park. In Newton Stewart you will find The Gem Rock Museum, A quaint old style cinema, as well as the Galloway Angling Centre.



History: The town was founded in mid 17th century, by William Stewart. The "New town of Stewart" was given burgh status by charter from King Charles II, which then allowed a weekly market, and two annual fairs to be held. Sir WIlliam Douglas (who founded the town Castle Douglas) establised cotton mills in Newton Stewart, which the was renamed "Newton Douglas" however the name did not stick, and they reverted back to "Newton Stewart".



Statistics: The population of Newton Stewart is 2'092
