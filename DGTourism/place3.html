Moffat

55.3352° N, 3.4403° W


About the area "Moffat"


Facts: Moffat stands for "The Long Plain". It is 59 miles south of Glasgow, and 51 miles south of Edinburgh, the capital. Moffat has many interesting things to do, Such as: Moffat Museum, The community nature reserve, and Station park.



History: The town is held to be the ancestral seat of "clan moffat" and then "Clan Johnstone" to hoard cattle that had been stolen in predatory raids. Moffat was notable for the market of wool trade, this has been commemorated with the statue of the ram in the towns market. The ram presented in 1875 by William Colvin.



Statistics: The population of moffat is 2'500.
