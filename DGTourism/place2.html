Thornhill

55.2459° N, 3.7691° W



About the area "Thornhill"


Facts: Thornhill is a town in the Mid Nithsdale area of Dumfries and Galloway, Scotland, south of Sanquhar and north of Dumfries on the main A76 road. Thornhill has many interesting things to do, such as: Drumlanrig Castle , Morton Castle and Dabton Loch provides amazing views for a picnic.



History: It was initially a small village, planned and built in 1717 on the Queensberry Estate on the road linking Dumfries to Glasgow. The Earl of Queensberry initially named the village 'New Dalgarnock' however the name did not achieve popular approval



Statistics: The population of Thornhill is 1'512
