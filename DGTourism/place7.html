About the area "Kirkcudbright"


Facts: The name of the early rendition of the towns current name was Kilcudbrit, which was derived from the gaelic translation meaning "chapel of Cuthburt". In kirkcudbright you will find: Cocoa Bean , Broughton House & Garden, and The Stewartry Museum.



History: In 1453 Kirkcudbright became a royal burgh. A century later, the magistrate of the town were given permission by Queen Mary to use part of the towns convent and nunnery, as a parish church. From 1570, Sir Thomas Maclellan of Bombie, who was Chief Magistrate, Was given a charter for the site, its ground and the gardens. The church was dismantled, in order to obtain material for his castle, which was to be built on the same site.



Statistics: The population of Kirkcudbright is 3'352
