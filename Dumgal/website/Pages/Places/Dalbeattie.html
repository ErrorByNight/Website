The building they found was owned by the British Legion but needed a lot of work done on it but Tommy Henderson agreed to rent it for 15 years on a pepper corn rent! The only condition of the rent was that he had to fund the renovations himself, so he then went about having Coffee Mornings, Craft Fairs, Charity shops to find the money for the development of the old store to a Museum. This family friendly forest on the Solway Coast has great facilities, scenic views, a pretty loch and abundant wildlife. Great mountain biking (part of 7 Stanes network) and footpaths dotted with features e.g. sculptures and an ancient hill fort. Dalbeattie is a town in the historical county of Kirkcudbrightshire in Dumfries and Galloway, Scotland. Dalbeattie is in a wooded valley on the Urr Water 6 km east of Castle Douglas and 19 km south west of Dumfries.
